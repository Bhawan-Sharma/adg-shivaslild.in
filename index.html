    Kasauli Trip

    September 22, 2021

    MY MEMORABLE TRIP TO THE MAGICAL LOCATION OF HIMACHAL PRADESH-KASAULI!!

    Kasauli is one of the most serene and beautiful destinations of Himachal Pradesh in the Solan Valley. Guarded by the snow-capped mountains, blooming streams, bright greeneries, meadows and covered in the blanket of dense timber wood forests, the destination has got everything. The antique old breathtaking Victorian mansions standing erect in the hilly surroundings under the calm environment gives the destination an alluring beauty as if descended straight from the heaven. And all the words I am having right now for its description is just because of my recent trip to Kasauli in the last weekend. It was so magical that during the entire trip it kept me drowning in its beauty. Friday I started my journey from Delhi on Friday afternoon from IBST Kashmiri gate and boarded my bus for Shimla route around 12 p.m. I had to get down at Kalka Bus station and I reached there at 7.15 p.m. covering a distance of 7 hours. The ambiance was fresh and evening cool air swept off my face giving a pleasant feeling. There were plenty of cabs for Kasauli at the bus stand and I took one that charged me Rs.1500 to my destination. The beautiful winding streets and the view of the hilly surroundings in the gloomy light were making me weak in the knees to control the excitement of my upcoming trip. I reached Kasauli around 10 p.m. in the night covering a duration of 1 ½ hour. I already had my booking at Homestead Villas in the army cantonment area and hence the cab dropped me directly there. I quickly finished my dinner and went off to sleep after the long journey. Saturday My first day trip began with Christ Church which is a very old and famous church of Kasauli in the Mall Road. Nothing is better than starting mornings with prayers in the abode of God. So after having my breakfast I went out for my trip. Christ Church It is the most famous tourist destination of Kasauli. The church has an awe-inspiring Victorian gothic architecture with green shack roofs and grey structure. The entrance has a big sundial leading to the hall decorated with stained glass windows along with an aisle reaching out to the altar where Jesus Christ is resting with Mother Mary and Joseph. The ambiance was so peaceful in the morning that I spend some quiet time offering prayers to Jesus and exploring the interiors. The exteriors gave me goosebumps when I got to discover that it holds the graves of all people during the period of 1850. The church is a pure combination of elegance and mystery. I had my lunch in a very cool hippie café known as Café Mantra. The interiors had a peppy feeling and I enjoyed eating some delicious pizzas.

    Sunset Point After lunch, my next destination was Sunset Point. I reached there around 4.30 p.m. The location has panoramic scenic beauty surrounded by pine vegetation and a beautiful walkway through the valleys popularly known as ‘Lovers Lane”. More I kept walking I felt magnetic towards its beauty under the twilight sky. The sunset had all red and golden hues everywhere in the location just like a paradise. I tried to capture the unparallel beauty in my camera lens as much as possible until it got dark and I finally had to leave for the day.

    Sunday A hill station trip is incomplete without trekking and hiking. So I decided to do some in the breathtaking locations of Kasauli. Sunset Point-Gilbert Trail-Manki Point I started from sunset point to Gilbert trail hiking in the upper area where the walkway shrinks from broad to narrow as you move forward. It was kind of an adventurous nature walk giving you a pleasant adventure amidst green surroundings and the misty snow-capped mountains. I continued my hiking up to Manki Point which took half an hour covering a distance of 1.7 km. It is another picturesque location having a beautiful Hanuman Temple and the biggest attraction is the hilltop that has the shape of Lord Hanuman’s feet. The location is one of the highest points of Kasauli and gives a mesmerizing view of the entire Chandigarh city from the hilltop. I was literally in cloud 9 reaching the hill-top and spend some time there admiring its beauty and capturing beautiful photographs. After a short hiking, I had my lunch at Café Rudra which is one the best eateries in Kasauli. It is famous for its North Indian and Chinese dishes and I decided to go Chinese this time with hot spicy Hakka noodles in the cool environment. After my sumptuous lunch, finally, it was time to bid goodbye to Kasauli. So, I hired a cab up to Chandigarh and from there took a bus in the evening for Delhi. The trip was very short but the experience was unparallel. It felt like someone took me from the real world to give a taste of paradise for two days. I would highly recommend this location to those travelers who dream of spending some quality time with dear ones in heavenly surroundings.

    Trip to Shimla & Mashobra

    September 17, 2021

    MY MONSOON TRIP TO THE PARADISE OF NORTH INDIA-SHIMLA AND MASHOBRA!!

    Shimla is one of the most beautiful hill stations of North India and the capital city of Himachal Pradesh. I have heard about it before from many people that, how the location is a perfect holiday destination adorned in Victorian architecture, snow-capped mountain ranges, and many more things until I visited it this time. Being a complete hill station lover, I will describe Shimla in one simple word “Paradise”. I always had a desire in my heart to visit Shimla once in my life but I never knew I would get the opportunity so soon. It seems like the destination kept on calling me until I planned my recent trip in the last weekend. Friday: Journey from Delhi to Shimla I started for my Shimla trip on Friday night. I reached to ISBT Kashmiri Gate around 6.45 p.m. and boarded my bus for Shimla from there. I am a comfort lover person so I always choose Volvo for long distance bus journeys. Saturday: Trip to Mashobra from Shimla After a long 9 hours 44 minutes bus journey, I finally arrived at Shimla bus stand around 4 a.m. in the morning. I felt refreshed when I stepped down and the first breeze of fresh air touched my face. I quickly hired a cab to reach Mashobra. It took nearly 35 minutes to reach Mashobra. Almost jumping with excitement I checked into my hotel immediately and slept for some time. I woke up around 9 a.m., had some breakfast and quickly got ready to step outside.

    A trip to the local villages I started my first day exploring the local villages of Mashobra. If I have to describe the beauty of the surroundings, a paintbrush, a canvas, and a palette of colors will be perfect. The entire town is absolutely breathtaking covered by Oak, Himalayan Deodar, Rhododendron, and Pine trees with majestic mountain ranges in the backdrop. Numerous colorful thatched roofs resting on the greeneries and cone-shaped roofs tops peeking amidst the quiet green surroundings made the entire area look heavenly. Most of the local village people here are the Pahari people speaking Pahari and Hindi language. The local culture here is very colorful with women wearing long knee length gowns and Salwar Kurta typically representing the Pahari culture. The people here make their living by agriculture and livestock. The weather was extremely cool and pleasant having frequent rainfalls. After each shower, the town seemed to be more vibrant just like a painted picture. I kept on capturing the beauty through my lens as long as I can. I literally couldn’t get enough of it. I felt a bit hungry after some time and decided to have my lunch. The food in Shimla is mostly North Indian cuisine and it was a delight to have some spicy food in the rainy weather. I enjoyed my lunch having grilled fish and Kullu trouts. The entire day went perfect gasping Mashobra’s beauty at each turn. I ended my day trip by evening and returned to my hotel. Sunday: Hike to Kufri My second-day destination was Kufri which is a small paradise in Shimla and famous for trekking and hiking. It was heavily raining outside, but I got so much engraved in nature's beauty that I hardly could think of anything. You can also drive by cab to Kufri but I opted for hiking. I crossed numerous stunning locations on my hiking trail until I reached Kufri. The destination offered panoramic views of the surrounding in the chilled monsoon weather. Next, I visited Indira Tourist Park located nearby. The park has a famous coffee shop called “Café Lalit”. I decided to spend my evening there relaxing with a hot cappuccino and some delicious snacks. I got drenched in rain miserably the entire day but I enjoyed too much trekking to Kufri. I checked out from Mashobra in Sunday evening and came back to Shimla. Monday: Trip to Mall Road and Christ Church I started early in the Morning with the beautiful Christ Church of Shimla. Christ Church The Church is a beautiful attraction among the picturesque backdrop of Shimla. It is an exemplary work of Neo-Gothic architecture dated back to 1857. The church is beautifully decorated with stained glass windows, a huge brass bell along with 5 tubular bells and an enormous seating arrangement of 630 people. I loved lighting a candle there and praying in the peaceful environment. After visiting Christ Church I went to the popular shopping destination of Shimla at the mall road. The Mall Road This is an amazing destination located at the heart of the Shimla. Surrounded by the mountain ranges the streets are lined up with numerous shopping centers, restaurants, banks, food stalls on either side. The area is also famous for numerous cafes serving delicious food housing two famous destinations-The Scandal Point and the Kali Bari. I enjoyed exploring all of them and spent the entire morning and afternoon covering the best spots of Shimla. Finally, my time was over, and I left for Delhi on Monday evening bidding goodbye to Shimla. My trip was short but it says little things give you the best enjoyment. I too had the same exploring beautiful town of Mashobra, its culture, food, and finally my favorite Shimla.
